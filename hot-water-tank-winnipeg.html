What is a Hot Water Tank? How does it work?

As mentioned above, the purpose of a hot water tank is to heat water, as well as store it for distribution throughout the household when needed. In order to accomplish this, all tanks are built with water supply and delivery pipes. The intake pipe, also known as the supply pipe, allows water to be drawn from your house to flow into the tank through a "dip tub." Meanwhile, the delivery pipe heats the water and distributes it to hot water outlets.

As hot water flows out of the tank through the top, your tank automatically refills with cold water at the bottom, which is then heated and stored for future use. A properly functioning tank will continually repeat this cycle as needed.

Hot water tanks are equipped with thermal insulation to reduce standby heat loss. This allows you to store hot water efficiently for a longer time. It is important that you ensure the thermal insulation of your hot water tank is adequate to keep hot water available when you require it. The tank's interior is lined with glass to protect against corrosion, as hot water tanks are constructed of steel.

Very low flow rate

To preserve hot water, many homeowners use showerheads with low flow rates to conserve their hot water. While great for this purpose, it can take longer for you to get it from your water heater. Low flow shower heads push the cold and old H20 more slowly through your water heater, until it is all expelled. Upgrading to a higher flow showerhead could be a simple solution for those who wish to receive their hot water faster, and could still be eco-friendly considering the waste that occurs during the wait period is now eliminated.
